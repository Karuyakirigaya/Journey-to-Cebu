    Cebu’s Historical Landmarks: A Journey Through Time

    Uncover Cebu’s rich historical past by visiting iconic sites like Fort San Pedro and Magellan’s Cross. Stroll through centuries of heritage at the Basilica del Santo Niño, or explore the Casa Gorordo Museum to glimpse into Spanish colonial life. Each landmark is a testament to Cebu’s significant role in shaping Philippine history.


    Step into Cebu’s past by exploring its historic landmarks, each telling a unique story of the island’s role in shaping Philippine history. From ancient sites of faith to colonial-era fortresses, every corner reveals a tale of courage, culture, and transformation.     Magellan’s Cross

    A historic landmark in Cebu City, Magellan's Cross marks the spot where Ferdinand Magellan planted a cross in 1521. It symbolizes the arrival of Christianity in the Philippines and is housed in a small chapel.

    Fort San Pedro
